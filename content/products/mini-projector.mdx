## The Promise
A projector you can literally put in your pocket. The pitch is compelling: movie nights anywhere, no setup, no cables.

## The Reality
This is not a home theater replacement. It's a dark-room companion. At 100 ANSI lumens, you need to close the curtains. But when you do...

The image is surprisingly sharp. 1080p on a 60-80 inch surface looks genuinely good. Colors are decent, not washed out like cheaper mini projectors.

## Best Use Cases
- **Bedroom movie nights**: Ceiling projection while lying in bed is a vibe
- **Camping**: The built-in 2-hour battery means no power cable needed
- **Kids' rooms**: Cartoons on the wall, instant babysitter

## The Downsides
The fan is noticeable during quiet dialogue scenes. No Bluetooth means you need wired speakers or headphones (3.5mm jack). And it needs darkness — even a small lamp kills the picture.

## Bottom Line
At $89.99, temper your expectations. It's a fantastic portable projector, not a cinema replacement. Perfect for casual viewing in controlled lighting. If that's your use case, you'll love it.
