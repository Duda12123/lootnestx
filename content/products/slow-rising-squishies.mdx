## Unboxing & First Impressions

They come in a simple polybag — no fancy packaging, just the squishies. The set we got included a hamburger, a donut with pink frosting, and what appears to be a piece of toast with a smiley face. They're about palm-sized, made from that distinctive slow-rise PU foam that feels cool to the touch.

First squeeze: pure dopamine. You squish the hamburger flat, and it takes a solid 4–5 seconds to slowly puff back to its original shape. It's hypnotic. The foam is soft but not sticky — it doesn't leave residue on your hands.

## Real-World Testing

### Desk Stress Test

Kept the donut on my desk for two weeks. Reached for it during Zoom calls, while reading long documents, and whenever I needed to think. It survived roughly 500+ squeezes with zero visible wear. The slow-rise rebound got slightly slower over time, but honestly, that just made it more satisfying.

### Kid Test

Handed the hamburger to a 6-year-old. Within 10 minutes it had been squeezed, thrown, sat on, and used as a pillow for a toy dinosaur. Zero damage. These things are tough.

## The Dust Problem

Here's the thing about slow-rise PU foam: it's a dust magnet. After a week on my desk, the donut had accumulated a visible layer of lint. You can rinse them with water to clean them, but they get a bit tacky when wet and take hours to fully dry. The best solution we found: store them in a small zip-lock bag when not in use.

## What Could Be Better

- A storage case would make a huge difference. Without one, these things WILL get dusty.
- The toast piece's smiley face started fading slightly after two weeks.
- Wish there were more savory food options (fries? pizza?).

## How It Compares

Versus cheap squishies from dollar stores, these are noticeably better — better foam density, more detailed paint, and they don't tear at the seams. Versus premium $25+ squishies from specialty shops, the quality is about 80% there at half the price.

## Bottom Line / Who Should Buy It

If you're a fidgeter, an anxious thinker, or just someone who likes having a tiny hamburger to squeeze during meetings — these are a delight. Great as a stocking stuffer or small gift. Not for people who freak out about dust.
